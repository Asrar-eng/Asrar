Question 1: How did John Snow move from correlation to causation?

•	Compared people who drank from the Broad Street pump with those who didn’t.
•	Showed infections dropped after the pump handle was removed.
Question 2: What if Snow used only hospital data?
•	He would miss many cases that never reached hospitals.
•	He might wrongly think the disease was spread evenly, not centered on the pump.
Question 3: A modern example of a “miasma.”
•	Belief that long work hours equal high productivity.
•	Assuming higher price always means higher quality.
